The Weekly Toolbox (WN33)

This is The Weekly Toolbox, week 33 of 2021. Linters and secrets management abound.

amber

A small secrets management program written in Rust. Created by people at FP Complete. Borne out from their Kubernetes deployment workflow. They have a blog post about amber and the repo is at Github.

I prefer the combination of sops and age. Just two Go programs to pass around. This project appears to use the Rust bindings to the sodium library. It’s built-in so just one executable to manage. An all-in-one secrets package similar to the sops + age combo. A good alternative indeed.

gokart

One thing I like about Go is the available linters and static analysis tools. I have used gosec before but it produced lots of false positives. A less eager cousin named gokart enters the field. According to the creators, it is smarter and more advanced than gosec. I think you should stack analysis tools into your CI or tests if you really care about security.

It’s very refreshing to see announcements about new projects from the creators themselves. I hope more people do it.
